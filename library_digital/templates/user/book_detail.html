Abstract & Summary

In "The Ethics of Aesthetics," Dr. Elena Sterling presents a revolutionary re-examination of our relationship with beauty in the digital age. Drawing from a vast archive of classical philosophy and contemporary cultural critique, Sterling argues that aesthetics are not merely a superficial veneer but the very scaffolding upon which our ethical frameworks are built.

The text meticulously navigates the tension between the curated digital self and the raw reality of the human condition. Through ten expansive chapters, the reader is invited to explore how our consumption of "perfected" imagery influences our capacity for empathy and communal responsibility.

This volume serves as a cornerstone for any serious student of modern philosophy, art history, or social science, providing a comprehensive bibliography and previously unpublished correspondence from the Sterling Archive.
